The Beginning

A Holiday That Became Home

In 2014, Maria and Wayan met on the Tegalalang rice terraces — she, an architect from Porto, wandering alone; he, a Balinese healer who had left his village to guide visitors through the sacred landscape of his ancestors.

Two years later, they began building Bali on land that had belonged to Wayan's family for four generations. Every stone was laid with intention. Every space designed to honour the spirit of place the Balinese call taksu.

In 2016, with seven villas and a small spa, Bali opened its doors. Today, with fifteen villas, three dining spaces, and a full wellness centre, the original spirit remains unchanged: this is not a hotel. It is a home. And you are family.

What We Stand For

Our Sacred Values

01

Tri Hita Karana

The Balinese concept of threefold harmony: harmony with God, with fellow humans, and with nature. The foundation of everything we do.

02

Community First

85% of our team are from surrounding Ubud villages. Every artisan, every farmer, every healer is local. Our success is theirs.

03

Radical Sustainability

Rainwater harvesting, solar power, organic gardens. We aim to return more to the earth than we take. Carbon neutral by 2026.

04

Genuine Luxury

Not the luxury of excess, but of presence. The luxury of silence, of extraordinary care, of being truly, deeply seen.

Our Journey

Eight Years of Growing

2014

First Meeting

Maria and Wayan meet on the Tegalalang rice terraces. An architect and a healer. The dream begins.

2015

Breaking Ground

Construction begins. Guided by traditional Undagi master builders. No concrete. Only volcanic stone and reclaimed teak.

2016

First Guests Arrive

Bali opens with 7 villas and a 3-room spa. Wayan's grandmother performs the first blessing ceremony.

2019

Awards & Recognition

Condé Nast Traveller Hot List. Travel + Leisure Top 100. Eight new villas and our treetop restaurant open.

2021

Wellness Centre Opens

The full spa sanctuary, hydrotherapy pool, and yoga pavilion complete the estate. Now 15 villas, 3 dining venues.

Today

Continuing the Journey

Still family-run. Still rooted. Still growing. Working towards full carbon neutrality and expanding our community programmes.
